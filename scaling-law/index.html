SCALING LAWS FOR NEURAL LANGUAGE MODELS · 13 equations, live

Loss doesn't wander.
It bends to a power law.

Kaplan et al. found that a language model's test loss is an astonishingly clean function of just three numbers: model size N, dataset size D, and compute C — each a straight line across seven orders of magnitude on a log-log plot. Below is every equation in the paper, explained parameter by parameter, and plotted live: move a slider and watch the law redraw itself.
